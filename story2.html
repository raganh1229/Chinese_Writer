Chinese Grammar Part 2

2018年 08月17日

1.) 了 – A model particle. It indicates that an action has occurred. It can appear either after a verb or at the end of a sentence. When it appears after a verb, it signals the occurrence of an action. There is usually a time phrase in the sentence.
Note: Immediately use after action.


Use 1.) Indicate a past action.
Ex.) 昨天我去对体育馆游泳。
Yesterday I went to swim in the gym.
Ex.) 我看了他的车。
I saw his car.


Use 2.) Notice that 了 is not the equivalent of the past tense. The action can take place in the future.
Ex.) 明天我会去了火车站。
Tomorrow I will go to the train station.


Use 3.) Sometimes there isn’t a time phrase in the sentence. The time implied is “just now” or “up till now.”
Ex.) 你买我们的票了吗？
Did you buy our tickets?
Ex.) 我买了。
I bought them.
Ex.) 等了多张时间？
Did you wait a long time?
Answer.) 人不多，只等了五分钟。
There were not a lot of people. I only waited five minutes.


Use 4.) If there is an object after the verb and 了, the object is usually quantified. Under certain circumstances, the object need not be quantified in any way.
Note: Used to remove the quantities of certain objects.
Ex.) 我买了票就回来家了。
I bought one ticket then returned home.
Ex.) 我给你打了电话了。
I have you a phone call.


Use 5.) When 了 occurs at the end of a sentence, it may signify a new situation, some kind of change, or the occurrence or realization of an event or state.
Note: Used to indicate a changing scenario.
Ex.) 十月了，天气慢慢冷了。
It’s October. The weather is gradually getting colder.
Ex.) 我想今天晚上看电话，可是明天要考试，所以不看了。
I wanted to go see a movie tonight, but I have an exam tomorrow, so I cannot see it.


Use 6.) When there are two verb phrases in a sentence and the first verb phrase is followed by the particle 了，the two actions denoted by the verbs are consecutive. The second action begins when the first one is completed.
Ex.) 我下了课再去找你。
I’ll look for you after class.
Ex.) 昨天般进了宿舍就去餐厅吃饭了。
Yesterday I finished moving into my dorm, then I went to eat at the cafeteria.
