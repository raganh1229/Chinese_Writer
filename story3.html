Chinese Grammar Part 3

2018年 08月 20日

1.) 是 + 什么什么 + 的 – Used to draw emphasis to an event or object. Note: 是 is often omitted.
Ex.) 他是什么时间来的？
What time did he come?
Ex.) 他们坐飞机来的还是开车来的？
Did they come by plane or by car?


2.) 除了 + 什么什么 + 以外 – Besides/in addition to.
3.) 除了 + 什么什么 + 以外，都 什么… - Except for.
Ex.) 除了我的狗了以外我有两只猫。
I have two cats in addition to my dog.
Ex.) 除了他有三辆车以外他有四辆摩托车。
In addition to his three cars, he additionally has four motorcycles.
Ex.) 除了我最朋友以外，我们班的同学都去过中国。
Except for my best friends, everyone in our class has been to China.
Ex.) 除了那本书以外，我都有很多东西。
I have a lot of things except for that book.


4.) 再说 – Use for additional reasons.
Ex.) 你别走了，天太晚了，再说我们要说的事还有说完呢。
Please stay, it’s getting late. Besides, we haven’t finished discussing everything we need to discuss.
Ex.) 我不要去日本。日本太贵了，再说我以前去过日本。
I don’t want to go to Japan. Things in Japan are too expensive; besides I’ve already been to Japan.


5.) 不但 + 什么什么 + 而且 – Also means besides, but unlike 再说, it’s not always explanatory.
Note: 不但 must be accompanied with 而且.
Ex.) 我还个学期不但上英文课，而且还上中文课。
This semester I not only have English class, but also Chinese class as well.
Ex.) 我姐姐不但喜欢唱歌，而且也喜欢跳舞。
My sister not only likes to sing, but also likes to dance.


6.）Existential Sentences – Indicates that something exists at a certain place.
Place word/phrase + v + 了/着/有/是 + numeral + measure word + noun.
Note: 有 distinguishes more than one objects. 是 denotes a singular object.
Use 1.） Used to indicate that something exists at a certain place.
Ex.) 桌子上放着一本书。
There is a book lying on the desk.
Ex.) 书桌的旁边是衣柜。
Next to the desk there is a wardrobe.
Ex.) 床前有一把椅子。
There is a chair in front of the desk.
Ex.) 教室里有很多学生。
There are a lot of students in the classroom.
Ex.) 桌子上是一张地图。
On the desk is a map.
Use 2.） Used to describe someone’s appearance or the surroundings of a location.
Ex.) 床上坐着一个人。
Someone is sitting on the bed.
Ex.) 桌子上有一本书，一份报和一些纸。
There’s a book, a newspaper, and some paper on the desk.


7.) 真 – Truly/really or to express a higher degree of emphasis.
Ex.) 我真喜欢了那电影！
I really liked that movie!
Note: 真 is used to express extreme emphasis. It should generally only be used to convey new information.
Ex.) 他真的我好朋友。
He is truly my good friend.
Note: Using 真 in this way is usually followed by 的.


8.) 得很 – Used after adjectives and certain verbs to denote thoughts or feelings to suggest an extreme extent.
Ex.) 学生刚开足额，大学都忙得很。
School just started. Everyone has been extremely busy.
Ex.) 来学校一个多兴星期了，我想家得很。
It has been over a week since I arrived on campus. I’m homesick.


9.) 那么 – Used to connect two sentences. The second sentence is a conclusion or judgement that derives from the preceding sentence.
Note：么 can often be omitted.
Ex.) 晚上我要去买东西，好吗？
Tonight I want to go buy some stuff. Is that okay?
Reply.) 可是今天晚上我没有空。
But tonight I don't have free time.
Reply.) 那（么）就明天吧。
Then we can go tomorrow.
